In 1950 there were three hundred and seventy-six million Indians. By 2023 there were one billion four hundred and twenty-nine million — and India had quietly overtaken China to become the most populous country on Earth. Numbers this large stop meaning anything, so we anchor them: every year for seven decades, India added a population the size of Australia.

What makes the story interesting is not the climb but the bend at the top — the moment the curve begins, almost imperceptibly, to flatten. Fertility has fallen below replacement. The India of 2060 is already visible in the slope of a single line.
