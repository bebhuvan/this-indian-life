# Indica V1 Question Spine

This is the national-first editorial spine for V1. The aim is not to publish every possible chart. The aim is to give a reader a rounded, evidence-backed sense of what India is: its scale, age, health, work, money, energy, air, climate exposure, state capacity, and everyday household reality.

V1 should start with national pages. State, district, and city pages come later as drilldowns.

## Editorial Rule

Every page should answer a public-interest question, not simply display an indicator.

Good:

- How many people live in India?
- Is India getting old before it gets rich?
- Why does GDP growth not feel like salary growth?

Weak:

- Population, total.
- GDP current US dollars.
- Labour force participation rate.

The question is the product surface. The indicator is the evidence.

## V1 Priority Levels

- **Core V1**: must ship in the first serious version.
- **V1+**: ship if the adapter is ready and the page can be made clear.
- **Later**: important, but requires harder source work, state/city drilldowns, or more careful methodology.

## What Not To Do In V1

- Do not start with 200 thin indicator pages.
- Do not start with state pages before the national spine is coherent.
- Do not build an open-data warehouse before publishing useful pages.
- Do not let LLM text create the analysis; it should explain locked numbers.
- Do not mix official and non-official sources without visible provenance.
- Do not hide caveats, especially for crime, unemployment, AQI, poverty, and projections.
