# Academy — Track 1: GDP & the National Accounts

The first track of the academy. The subject is *the ruler*, not the thing being
measured: what GDP is, how **India** computes it, and what the number hides.

**Sequencing rule.** Each entry assumes only the ones before it, introduces a
handful of new terms at most, and cashes out every abstract term in a real Indian
number before it ends. Nothing is allowed to stay abstract.

**Format.** Every entry is one answerable question = one page = one slug. Slugs are
stable; numbers are for discussion only and will drift as we insert. Each entry's
prose is generated from a locked evidence packet (so figures can't be invented) and
edited against `VOICE.md`.

**Data anchors** point at series we likely already have (`econ.nas.*` from the MoSPI
NAS ingest, plus World Bank / IMF for the world lens). Anchors marked *(check)* need
a series confirmed or ingested before that entry can be generated.

## The flagship structure (the curriculum, inverted — decided 2026-06-06)

The 116 atomic questions above are no longer 116 standalone pages. They are a
**section-bank**: the atomic questions become the H2 reader-questions *inside* a small
set of deep, question-led flagship entries (the "Quanta for India" format). The GDP
track resolves to ~7 flagships, plus a few short cards for genuinely atomic terms.

GDP-track flagships:

1. **What is GDP, really?** — `what-is-gdp` — DONE. The keystone overview: what it is,
   where it came from, how India computes it, what it tells/hides, the measurement debate.
2. **Why does India headline GVA, not GDP?** — `gva-vs-gdp` — GVA vs GDP, the three price
   tags (factor cost / basic / market), product taxes, the bridge, the 2015 switch.
3. **What does India actually make?** — `structural-change` — the three sectors, the
   shift from farms to services, the skipped factory stage, the output-vs-jobs gap.
4. **Who spends India's money?** — `who-spends` — the expenditure side (PFCE/GFCF/GFCE/NX),
   why India is consumption-led, the investment rate.
5. **Big country, poor people** — `big-but-poor` — per-capita, GDP vs GNI, PPP vs market,
   why the average hides inequality.
6. **How is GDP measured, and why does the number keep changing?** — `how-gdp-is-measured`
   — who computes it, the AE/PE/RE revision cycle, base years, the back-series fight.
7. **What GDP can't see** — `what-gdp-ignores` — distribution, unpaid work, environment,
   the informal economy (deeper than the what-is-gdp section).

Short cards (kept atomic, ~300-600w): `what-gross-means` (DONE), and similar single-term
explainers as needed. Each flagship's brief lives in `docs/academy/briefs/<slug>.json`
with a `sectionArc`, `featureNumbers`, vetted `contextCards`, and `depth: "flagship"`.
